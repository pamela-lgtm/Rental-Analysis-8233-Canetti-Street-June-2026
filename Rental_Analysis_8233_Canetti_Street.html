02 · The Location

Laureate Park — and why "the Preserve" is a different market

Laureate Park is the urban-grid core of Lake Nona in 32827 — walkable to Lake Nona Town Center and Medical City, and built around an exceptional amenity package: the Aquatic Center and resort-style pools, the LP Fit gym, the Lake House event venue, community gardens, a dog park, and more than forty miles of trails. Phase 1B, where this home sits, is one of the original, central phases — close to the heart of that amenity grid, which is consistently where tenant demand is strongest.

It's also genuinely central to the things that drive Lake Nona's renter pool: Medical City, the VA and Nemours hospitals, the USTA campus, Orlando International Airport, and the 417/528 corridor. That demand base — relocating healthcare professionals, corporate transfers, airline crews — is exactly the well-qualified, long-term tenant this investment should attract when it's presented correctly.

The Preserve at Laureate Park — why it commands more

You may come across a recent Laureate Park lease at $7,200 — and it's worth understanding why it isn't a true comp for your home, so we've left it out of the comp set entirely. That home is in The Preserve at Laureate Park — Laureate Park's only gated enclave, on the northern end, built by Taylor Morrison on the largest, oversized lots in the community, with larger custom and semi-custom floor plans (up to ~4,950 sq ft) and several lakeside sites. Many sell north of $1M. It carries a genuine privacy-and-prestige premium that the non-gated Laureate Park homes that make up the rest of the community — including this one — simply do not, and pricing 8233 Canetti against it would set a number the market will never pay.

What the working set tells us

9112 Yonath ($4,300) is the anchor. A 5/4.1 of nearly identical size (3,620 sq ft) and age (2016) in Laureate Park — the truest match to this home. Notably, it asked $4,750 and had to settle at $4,300, taking 26 days to do it. That's the central lesson: overpricing a strong home doesn't get you more, it gets you a longer vacancy and a price cut anyway.

8549 Laureate Blvd ($3,750) marks the lower band. It leased at full ask but took 54 days — a classic sign of a home that was either priced toward the bottom or didn't present at the top of the market. Either way, it shows where the floor sits and how a less-than-pristine presentation slows things down.

9421 Dugard ($5,800 asking) is a caution, not a comp. It's unproven — newly listed and not yet leased. We'll learn from whether it sits, but we don't price against a number no tenant has agreed to.

On the $4,900 listing: Bull's current listing shows the home furnished with cable and internet bundled in — but since it will be offered unfurnished (Section 01), that figure isn't the benchmark for this placement. Even taken at face value, a furnished, services-included rate carries a higher headline number by design while trading that premium for a far smaller long-term tenant pool (most quality long-term renters own their furniture) and added owner liability. Backing those inclusions out, it lands right in line with the unfurnished working set — so it doesn't change the recommendation below.

Why this isn't the 2023 number

This home leased at $4,500 in 2023, so it's fair to ask why the recommendation isn't higher three years on. (That 2023 listing also showed as furnished — but, as with the current listing, we can't confirm that was accurate, so we treat it as an unfurnished lease.) The reason the number isn't higher is supply: since 2023, builders have released a substantial wave of new construction across Lake Nona, and that added competition has pressured rents on existing homes; tenants simply have more choices today than they did then. An unfurnished placement in today's market lands at or just below that 2023 figure — not above it. That's an accurate read, not a discount, and pricing as if 2023 conditions still hold is one of the surest ways to make a home sit.

The line we do not cross

Avoid the $4,750–$5,800 zone. Yonath asked $4,750 and the market pushed it down to $4,300; Dugard is sitting at $5,800 unleased. For a non-gated Laureate Park home like this one, pricing into that range is how you guarantee a long vacancy — the opposite of the goal.

05 · Condition Is Everything

At this price point, presentation is the whole game

There is no meaningful "average" days-on-market for a Lake Nona home, because the number is driven by condition and price, not the calendar. A home that is pristine and priced correctly leases inside 21 days — often far faster. A home that is dated or shows poorly will sit for weeks at any price, while the owner absorbs the vacancy. The well-qualified tenant this investment should attract expects the home to be immaculate, and increasingly expects hard flooring over carpet. To command the best resident, a rental investment has to stay current with updates and, at minimum, be flawless in its presentation.

A real, recent benchmark

Last week we leased a home in the Isles of Lake Nona in two days, at $3,995 — a 2023-built, single-story 4/3 with a three-car garage and water views — to a highly qualified renter relocating with a major airline. It moved that fast because it was pristine and priced right. That is the standard this market rewards, and it's the standard we hold every listing to.

06 · Your Questions, Answered

Directly to the points you raised

What rental rate would the home obtain?

Unfurnished and show-ready, the working comparables support roughly $4,300–$4,550, and we'd recommend launching at $4,395. The $4,900 you're seeing now is a furnished rate with cable and internet bundled in — a different product that isn't directly comparable.

What's the average vacancy between tenants?

Honestly, there isn't a reliable average — and any manager who quotes you one is guessing. Vacancy length is a function of two things you control: condition and price. A pristine, correctly priced home leases inside 21 days, frequently faster (our recent Isles lease took two). A turn between an outgoing and incoming tenant then depends only on the make-ready scope — typically a short, defined window, not an open-ended gap.

Won't I get stuck off the summer leasing cycle?

That risk comes from slow lease-ups, not from the calendar. When a home leases in two-to-three weeks, you stay on the cycle you want — and you can align the lease term so renewals and turnovers land in the stronger spring/summer window going forward. Leasing fast is precisely how we keep you on-cycle rather than off it.

How long do Laureate Park homes stay vacant once available?

The well-presented ones in the heart of Laureate Park move quickly — demand here is deep. The ones that linger are the over-priced or under-presented (you can see it in the comps: Yonath had to cut its price; the $3,750 home still took 54 days). Location helps, but condition and price decide it.

What fees apply between tenants, beyond the 50% leasing fee?

The full, transparent fee structure — including turnover and make-ready — is laid out in the Lake Nona Owner Guide, alongside our FAQs and a sample management agreement, which is a standard document we do not modify. One point worth flagging now, since you're thinking about turnover cost: the single best way to avoid turnover expense is to retain a great tenant, which is why our renewal fee is a flat $200 — a fraction of the cost of re-leasing. Keeping a qualified resident in place is both the cheapest and the lowest-risk outcome, and it's what we optimize for.
